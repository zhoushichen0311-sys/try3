ABOUT US

RC5 – Mnemosyne Team
The Bartlett School of Architecture, UCL

Our research focuses on aging societies and the spatial challenges faced by elderly communities. As physical ability and daily routines change over time, conventional housing often fails to adapt to the evolving needs of its residents. We ask a fundamental question:

Can architecture behave like a product—capable of adapting, updating, and reconfiguring according to the user?

To address this, we developed the Mnemosyne System, a design framework that transforms architectural space into a modular, responsive living product. By integrating spatial modules with user-based data, the system can automatically generate and recombine living environments tailored to the changing physical conditions of elderly residents.
